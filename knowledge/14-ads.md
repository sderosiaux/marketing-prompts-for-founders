# Paid Advertising for SaaS Founders

## Overview

Paid ads are structured experiments, not ongoing spend. Set duration upfront (12 weeks for LinkedIn, 3-6 months for Google), measure against clear KPIs, allocate budget across funnel stages, and treat each channel separately. Never sole acquisition source.

---

## When to Use Ads (vs Organic)

**Right to run ads when:**
- Clear ICP defined with validated demand
- Product-market fit established or near
- $3K-5K minimum monthly budget (3+ months)
- Landing page optimized (pain point → solution → proof → offer)
- Conversion tracking set up (hard: demo/trial; soft: page views)

**Too early**: Pre-PMF stage, insufficient budget, no attribution infrastructure

---

## Platform Selection Framework

| Platform | Best For | Minimum Budget | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Google Ads** | Bottom-funnel, high-intent keywords | €25-50/day ($750+/mo) | 30 days first conversions; 3-6 months visibility |
| **LinkedIn** | B2B awareness, decision-maker targeting | $3K/month | 12-week test window; 30 days first conversions |
| **Facebook/Meta** | Remarketing, awareness, broad audiences | $500-1K/week | Ongoing optimization |
| **Reddit** | Niche communities, developer audiences | $500+/week | Test across subreddits |

**Key difference**: Google targets "what people search" (intent). LinkedIn targets "who people are" (role, company, seniority).

---

## Multi-Stage Budget Allocation (50-30-20 Rule)

Allocate across funnel regardless of platform:

- **Awareness (50-55%)**: Problem identification, brand lift (company lists, memes, product demos)
- **Consideration (30%)**: Solution validation (testimonials, reviews, partnerships, comparisons)
- **Conversion (15-20%)**: Direct CTAs, limited offers, demos/trials

---

## Google Ads Execution

**Campaign Structure** (by intent):
1. Competitor keywords (20-30% budget): "[Competitor] alternative," "[Competitor] competitors"
2. Product category (60-70% budget): "Sales automation software," "AI research tools"
3. Use case keywords (included above): "Transcribe Zoom calls," "Automate lead nurturing"
4. Brand (20-25% budget): Your brand name, misspellings, competitor brand variants

**Setup Checklist:**
- Google Tag Manager (GTM) for centralized tracking
- Conversion tracking: form submissions, demo requests, trial signups
- Google Consent Mode V2 (EU/UK compliant)
- CRM integration for offline conversions (MQLs, SQLs, customers)
- Max 2 campaigns initially; 1 ad group per creative theme
- Use Responsive Search Ads (RSAs) with 15 headlines, 4 descriptions

**Negative Keywords to Exclude:**
- "Free [product]"
- Generic informational queries
- Non-target industries/use cases

**Remarketing Setup:**
- Target users who visited site in 30 days without converting
- Use display network and Gmail ads
- Creative: comparison pages, case studies
- Connect GA4 + enable Consent Mode

**Benchmarks:**
- CTR: 3-5%
- Conversion rate (demo/trial): 1-1.5%+
- Free trial CVR: 7%+
- CPA: Set based on LTV (€120-500 typical)

## LinkedIn Ads Execution

**12-Week Experiment Model:**
Budget formula: (Target conversions × Target CAC) = Total spend
Example: 200 demos × $100 CAC = $20K investment

**Campaign Structure:**
- Single experiment campaign with one ad set
- 5-6 weekly rotating creatives (pause old ones before launching new to force algorithm recalibration)
- Once positive ROAS: split into 90% main + 10% test budget

**Audience Targeting:**
- Niche audiences: 10-50k people (opposite of Meta's broad approach)
- Target by job roles, seniority, company size
- Use reverse exclusion (exclude 51+ employee companies vs. targeting 1-50)
- Disable audience network expansion

**Bidding:**
- Manual bidding with cost caps
- Start 50% below LinkedIn's recommended bid
- Increase 10% every 24-48 hours until reaching full daily spend

**Creative Testing Roadmap:**
- Weeks 1-6: Launch new ad creatives weekly
- Week 7+: Test audiences and campaign types with top performers

**High-Performing Creative Elements:**
- Large, bold numbers/metrics
- Customer testimonials and case studies
- Avoid stock photos and product screenshots
- Single-image ads before testing video (4:5 ratio for images, 1:1 for video)

**Benchmarks:**
- Single image: CTR > 0.3%, dwell time > 3 sec, quality score > 5
- Video: 30%+ view rate, 10%+ completion rate
- Average CPC: $15-100 range
- Conversion funnel: 15% click-through (to landing page) × 22% landing conversion (to form)
- 222 touchpoints + 2,627 impressions needed to close deals (69-day average sales cycle)

**Common Mistakes:**
- Running 1-week campaigns with <$500 budget
- Testing multiple variables simultaneously
- Using automatic audience expansion
- Splitting budget across too many GTM channels before mastering 1-2
- Mixing top-funnel and bottom-funnel creatives
- Failing to pause previous ads before launching new experiments
- Underestimating budget (minimum $3K/month over 3 months)

---

## Creative Performance Hierarchy

**Top Performers** (all platforms):
- Large, bold numbers/metrics
- Customer testimonials and quotes
- Before/after comparisons
- Problem-centric headlines (BBB rule: Bold Background, Bold headline, single Benefit)
- Clear, single benefit per ad

**Bottom Performers:**
- Stock photography
- Product dashboard screenshots
- Generic value propositions
- Overly salesy copy
- Multiple CTAs in single ad

---

## Universal Anti-Patterns

1. **Under-budgeting**: <$3K/month over 3 months fails; $500/week campaigns underperform
2. **Testing too many variables at once**: Change one variable per batch
3. **Poor creative execution**: Use metrics, testimonials, raw content; avoid generic copy
4. **Incomplete setup**: Missing conversion tracking, audience expansion enabled, wrong bidding
5. **Early shutdowns**: Minimum 30 days to first conversions; 3-6 months for pipeline visibility
6. **Sole-channel dependency**: Paid is complementary, not standalone

---

## Measurement Across All Platforms

**Minimum reporting metrics:**
- Impressions, clicks, CTR
- Conversion rate (soft and hard)
- Cost per conversion (CPC/CPA)
- ROAS (revenue or pipeline value)

**Key insight**: Don't just track last-touch attribution. Track hard conversions twice: last-touch campaign AND overall last-touch.

---

## Pre-Launch Checklist

- [ ] Conversion tracking installed (GTM + CRM)
- [ ] Landing page optimized (pain point → solution → proof → offer)
- [ ] Audience defined (ICP, roles, companies, OR exclusions)
- [ ] Budget calculated (conversions × CAC = spend)
- [ ] Creative assets prepared (5-6 variations minimum)
- [ ] Reporting template created
- [ ] Cost caps and bidding strategy set

**During Campaign (Weeks 1-6):**
- [ ] Weekly creative rotations (pause old, launch new)
- [ ] Daily bid adjustments (10% increase every 24-48h)
- [ ] Monitor CTR, conversion rate, CPC
- [ ] Collect data before optimization

**After 30 Days:**
- [ ] First conversions appear
- [ ] Identify top 20% performers
- [ ] Scale winners; pause bottom performers

**After 3-6 Months:**
- [ ] Pipeline visibility established
- [ ] ROAS positive = readiness to scale
- [ ] Consider channel diversification
