# Content Marketing for SaaS Founders

## Overview

Content marketing works by leading with value, building trust through distribution, then converting. The core principle: **add value every time**. Match content types to buyer awareness stage, structure for SEO and repurposing, and make distribution equal to creation effort.

---

## Content Strategy by Market Position

Choose your starting point based on market maturity:

| Market Type | Start Here | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **Mature** | Product Awareness | Competitive comparisons, differentiators |
| **Emerging** | Category Awareness | Why this category beats alternatives |
| **New** | Use Case Awareness | Problem education, new approaches |

Create 5-10 pieces per stage before advancing. Use this progression within a single content pillar.

---

## Content Types by Funnel Stage

**Outcome Awareness** (widest): Glossaries, formulas, statistics, tip lists, difference breakdowns
- Lowest barrier to discovery; targets early-stage awareness
- AI-generated content works well here

**Use Case Awareness**: How-to guides, step-by-step processes, templates, best practices
- Highest conversion potential of early-stage content
- Build learning paths linking multiple tutorials

**Product Category Awareness**: Comparison lists, ultimate guides, swipe files, resource collections
- Education on solution types vs. alternatives
- Differentiate through depth (include templates, frameworks, examples)

**Product Awareness**: Deep dives, direct competitor comparisons, multi-way comparisons, case studies
- Target searchers already evaluating
- High human input; AI amplification only

**Purchase Decision**: Buying checklists, role-specific guides, customer testimonials, customer success stories
- Require practitioner/customer involvement
- Build credibility through authentic voices

## Execution Framework

**1. Foundation Work (Before Creating)**
- Define ICP and 3 core perceptions to shift
- Map customer goals from sales calls + support tickets
- Establish competitive positioning and messaging
- Create 2-3 content pillars aligned to buyer jobs

**2. Content Production**
- Create one strong piece per month minimum
- Pair with 50+ social variations from single piece
- Build internal linking for "binge reading" discovery
- Add sticky table of contents and learning paths for long-form content

**3. Distribution (Equal Effort to Creation)**
- 8 hours per piece: 4 hours creation, 4 hours distribution
- Three-step model: external platform → owned channels → email list
- Concentrate effort on 1-2 platforms initially ("50 retweets become 500")

**Platform-Specific Tactics:**
- **Hacker News/Reddit**: Speed to publication + depth, minimal self-promotion, timing advantage
- **LinkedIn**: "Comment below" founder posts distributing lead magnets, engagement before algorithms
- **Blog**: SEO for long-tail keywords, internal linking, sticky navigation, learning path structure
- **Email**: Build newsletter simultaneously (Loops, Customer.io recommended); not an afterthought

## Lead Magnets: Value Tier System

Conversion impact hierarchy (highest to lowest):

1. **Tactical** (templates, checklists, calculators, examples) = Highest conversion
   - Example: Webinar ROI calculator, cold email templates, equity dilution calculator
   - Solves immediate workflow problem

2. **Educational** (guides, ebooks, webinars, courses) = Moderate conversion
   - Example: Product selling masterclass, video creation course
   - Builds knowledge progressively

3. **Inspirational** (industry reports, research, case studies) = Lowest conversion but builds authority
   - Example: State of meetings report, SaaS GTM reports
   - Establishes thought leadership

**Distribution Methods:**
- Value-first outreach (send resource before pitching)
- Secondary CTAs/banners on website
- Founder "comment below" LinkedIn posts
- Embedded on blog post CTAs
- Micro-influencer partnerships
- Social media ads (LinkedIn thought leadership focus)

Key: Specific, relevant content for ICP. Distribution equals creation effort.

## Newsworthy Content Strategy

Identify breaking industry news, then create timely, value-added analysis:

1. State the newsworthy item with source link
2. Explain underlying problem
3. Detail implications for your audience
4. Forecast future developments
5. Conclude with minimal self-promotion (one sentence max)

**Execution**: Automate news monitoring (free newsbots available), reach out to journalists before publishing, submit to HN/Reddit simultaneously for multi-channel distribution.

**Benchmarks**: 150-400 backlinks from 50-90 domains per newsworthy piece.

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Creating content without distribution plan (most common failure)
- Excessive marketing language (kills trust on HN/Reddit)
- Forcing rigid calendars instead of responding to ecosystem changes
- "Perpetual content hunger"—overfilling calendars without distribution capacity
- Ignoring audience-specific norms (Reddit ≠ LinkedIn ≠ Twitter)
- Over-relying on AI volume without strategic depth
- Starting without ICP definition and messaging framework
- Creating scattered content across awareness stages instead of focused programs
- Skipping newsletter building (owned channel)
- Expecting organic distribution without community engagement

---

## Measurement

**Add to lead forms**: "How did you hear about us?" with content/social source options

**Track these metrics**:
- Newsletter subscriber growth (baseline: 0 → 19k in 1 year without paid ads is achievable)
- Backlinks acquired (150-400 from 50-90 domains per newsworthy piece)
- Revenue cohorts by content source
- First-month activation/churn by channel
- Content-to-conversion attribution (gated content = higher value)

---

## Recommended Starting Framework

1. Define ICP + 3 core perception shifts
2. Choose one awareness stage based on market maturity
3. Create 5-10 pieces matching that stage (blog, lead magnet, LinkedIn, newsletter)
4. Plan distribution first (8 hours repurposing per piece)
5. Concentrate on 1-2 platforms initially, then expand
6. Build newsletter simultaneously
7. Measure attribution carefully
8. Iterate based on feedback, not guesses
