# Cold Outreach & Sales

## Overview
Cold outreach is a relevance game, not a numbers game. Quality targeting + trigger events + authentic messaging + strategic sequencing drives predictable customer acquisition. Solo founders should validate approach with 50 hand-picked leads before scaling to thousands. This playbook covers prospecting through demo to close.

## Frameworks

### Prospect Identification: ICP Development
**ICP Development Process**:
1. Start with Early Customer Profiles (ECP) as ICP proxy: Tech Enthusiast, Underdog Supporter, Game Changer, Digital Transformer, Agent of Change
2. Define ICP across 4 dimensions: Job titles (decision-makers + influencers), Company industry (vertical focus), Company size (headcount range), Location/timezone
3. Build Buying Committee Models: Identify Economic Buyer, Technical Buyer, User Buyer, Champion roles
4. Create Anti-ICP Framework: Define characteristics to avoid (long sales cycles, low ACV, high churn, support-heavy)

**Minimum Data for ICP Analysis**:
- Enterprise (€100k+ ACV): 5+ customers
- Mid-market (€25–100k): 25+ customers
- SMB (€5–25k): 100+ customers
- Prosumer (<€5k): 100+ customers

### Trigger Events: Buying Signals
Trigger events signal immediate buying readiness:
- **Job changes within 90 days** (fresh budgets, new priorities)
- **Testimonials given to competitors** (dissatisfaction signal)
- **New GTM/RevOps/Revenue hire** (team expansion = buying intent)
- **Active job postings** (team expansion = budget allocation)
- **Recent funding rounds** (capital deployment)
- **Website redesigns** (modernization momentum)
- **Tech stack changes** (platform switching)
- **Company headcount growth** (tool need expansion)

### Outreach Playbook: Three-Level Model

**Level 1 – Manual & Hyper-Personal (Validation)**
- 50 hand-picked leads per campaign
- Goal: Validate ICP, triggers, messaging hooks
- Expected conversion: 40% of contacted → demo (20 demos from 50 contacts)
- Duration: 1–2 weeks
- Stop here before scaling

**Level 2 – Semi-Automated (Growth)**
- 1,000–2,000 contacts via Instantly (email) or Heyreach (LinkedIn)
- Contextual personalization at scale
- Expected: 3–5% positive interest rate
- Multi-channel approach (LinkedIn + email)

**Level 3 – Engine Mode (Scaling)**
- Refined ICP + tested message + proven proof assets
- Multi-touch campaigns across 3–5 channels
- Data stack integration (Clay for prioritization, trigger detection)
- Expected: 10,000+ leads/month at <$0.10 cost per lead

## Playbook: Find Leads → Craft Message → Sequence → Follow Up → Demo → Close

### Step 1: Find Leads
**LinkedIn Sales Navigator Tactics**:
- Create saved searches as "24/7 prospecting assistant"
- Reach out within 24 hours of profile view (highest interest window)
- Target job changers within 90 days exclusively
- Engage with prospects' content before connecting
- Use Boolean search: ("VP of Sales" OR "Head of Sales") NOT Assistant
- Build separate lists: top 20 accounts, competitive opportunities, fast-scaling
- Verify emails before export; use Scalelist, Lemlist, Surfe for enrichment

**Tools**: LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Clay (data enrichment), PredictLeads (monitor 92M companies, 100 free credits/month)

**Email Verification**:
- Categorize: Valid (safe), Risky (catch-all, 20–30% may work), Invalid (skip)
- Use ZeroBounce or similar for bounce/spam trap detection
- Split mailboxes 50/50 GSuite + Outlook for deliverability

### Step 2: Craft Message (3-Message Sequence)

**Message 1: Hook + Trigger** (Keep short, human-written)
- Personal, relevant opener stating intent
- One-liner explaining who you are/what you do
- Context showing why you're worth responding to
- No demo links in initial outreach
- Establish "why now?" through trigger event reference

**Template structure**:
```
Hi [Name],

[Specific observation about their company/situation]

[Who you are + one-liner of what you do]

[Why this is relevant to them now — show credibility through case study/result]

[Soft CTA — ask question or suggest conversation]

[P.S. — soft secondary CTA or value add]
```

**Tone formula**:
- Casual-professional hybrid (peer-to-peer, not seller-to-prospect)
- Use contractions (you're, let's, don't)
- Include specific numbers: "increased from $0 to $55k in 3 months"
- Ask hypotheses rather than make statements: "If I'm seeing this right..."
- Acknowledge uncertainty: "You might have noticed..."
- End with thoughtful questions, not statements
- Avoid: Corporate jargon, absolute claims, arrogance, generic templates, AI-sounding copy

**Example (Good tone)**:
```
Hey Will—Your site gets a ton of traffic (congrats!), but I noticed your subscriber list isn't keeping pace. Got a minute to discuss strategies to bridge that gap?
```

**Example (Avoid)**:
```
Good morning Mr. Jones, My analysis of your website traffic indicates healthy search volumes; however, subscriber acquisition appears suboptimal.
```

**Message 2: Value Drop** (3 days later)
- Share resource proving success with similar company
- Ask thoughtful question about their situation
- Continue problem-first approach
- Add fresh value, don't repeat message 1

**Message 3: Follow-Up CTA** (7 days later)
- "Worth a look?" style follow-up
- Add fresh value, don't repeat
- Stop at 3 touches to avoid spam perception

**Follow-Up Timing**:
- Message 1 → Wait 3 days → Message 2 → Wait 4 days → Message 3
- Send follow-up emails within 24 hours after meetings/calls
- Maximum 3 total touches per prospect

### Step 3: Email Sequence & Infrastructure

**Email Infrastructure Setup**:
- Never send cold email from primary domain (reputation damage)
- Use secondary domains redirecting to main domain
- Deploy 3 mailboxes per domain maximum
- Distribute across multiple domains to isolate reputation damage

**Warmup Period (14 days)**:
- Join warmup pool with 1M+ accounts (e.g., Instantly)
- Establish engagement signals before cold campaigns
- Build IP/domain credibility gradually

**Sending Limits**:
- Start at 5 emails/day per mailbox
- Gradually increase to max 30/day per mailbox
- Account for follow-up sequences in limit calculations (3x per lead)

**Tier 1 Setup (Validation)**:
- 3 domains, 9 mailboxes total
- 5,000 emails/month capacity
- Cost: $109–258/month
- Cost per lead: $0.15

**Tier 2 Setup (Growth)**:
- 20 domains, 60 mailboxes
- 10,000+ leads reached monthly
- Cost: ~$950/month
- Cost per lead: $0.095

**Domain Setup (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)**:
- All three authentication protocols take <1 hour per domain
- Essential for inbox placement

### Step 4: Demo & Sales Call

**9-Phase Demo Structure**:
1. **Research & Preparation** (10–15 min prep before call)
2. **Warm-up & Introduction** (3–5 min)
3. **Framing the Demo** (1–2 min)
4. **Discovery or Recap** (10–15 min)
5. **Buy-in on Pain Points** (1 min)
6. **Feature Demonstration** (15–20 min, 3–4 features max)
7. **Reconfirmation** (1 min)
8. **Pricing Discussion** (3–5 min)
9. **Summary & Next Steps** (3–5 min)

**Optimal Metrics**:
- Total duration: 15–30 minutes (30 min absolute max)
- Presenter talk time: ≤50% (prospect ≥50%)
- Features shown: 3–4 maximum
- Discovery phase: 10–15 minutes minimum
- Follow-up sent: Within 24 hours

**Discovery Questions**:
- "Tell me about your current priority?"
- "How do you currently handle [XYZ topic]?"
- "What's holding you back from solving this?"
- "What would an ideal result look like in 6 months?"

**Pain-to-Value Mapping (DAIC Model)**:
- **Discover**: Ask questions to uncover pain
- **Awareness**: Ensure prospect recognizes the problem
- **Implication**: Highlight negative consequences of status quo
- **Confirm**: Get prospect agreement on impact

**Pricing Conversation Transition**:
"I've shown how we solve [pain point] and move you toward [outcome], which is tremendous value. Now let's discuss pricing."

**Demo Close Options**:
- "What will it take for you to become a customer?"
- "Unless you have questions, I think we're ready to get started."

### Step 5: Follow-Up Emails After Calls

**Post-Initial Contact Email** (5 sections):
1. Email opener
2. Observation/recap
3. Value proposition
4. Next steps/CTA
5. P.S. (soft CTA)

**Post-Discovery Call Email** (6 sections):
1. Email opener
2. Recap discovery (status quo, pain points, desired outcomes)
3. Value proposition (what you do, for whom, benefits, social proof)
4. Blocker/objection call-outs
5. Next steps (agreed roadmap)
6. P.S./secondary CTA

**Post-Demo Call Email** (6 sections):
1. Email opener
2. Recap of discovery insights
3. Value proposition (personalized to prospect needs)
4. Proactive blocker/objection handling
5. Agreed next steps
6. P.S./secondary CTA

## Email Templates

### Cold Email Template: Hook + Credibility Pattern
```
Hi [Name],

Since [company] offers [product/service], I thought you'd be interested in [specific observation showing research].

What is your team doing to [solve specific problem]? [Proof from similar company showing results]

Are you interested in chatting about [topic]?

[Your name]
P.S. [Soft secondary value add or CTA]
```

### High-Response Follow-Up Pattern
```
Hi [Name],

Following up on my earlier message. I thought you might find this useful: [link to case study/resource/video showing relevant solution].

[Ask thoughtful question about their specific situation]

Would a quick conversation make sense?

[Your name]
```

### Tone Example (Casual-Professional)
```
Hi Sarah,

Noticed your company just hired a VP of Marketing (congrats on the growth!). Usually that's when teams realize their current reporting tool is holding them back.

We help teams like yours cut reporting time by 70%. Here's how a similar company at your stage did it: [link].

Curious—how are you currently handling your reporting workflow?

Best,
[Your name]
```

## Demo Playbook

See Step 4 above for complete 9-phase structure. Key points:
- Lead with questions, not statements
- Alternate: Show feature → Discuss benefit → Ask question
- Map Point A (status quo) to Point B (desired outcome)
- Use real-world examples and customer testimonials
- Reconfirmation statement before pricing: "So what I understood is that you [current situation] but feel unsatisfied because [pain points]. Your ideal scenario is [desired outcome]. Correct?"

## Benchmarks

**Activity Metrics**:
- LinkedIn connection acceptance: 30%+
- Email open rate: 50%+
- Email bounce rate: <5%
- Multi-channel response rate: 10%+

**Conversion Metrics**:
- Hand-picked targeting: 40% of contacted should have demo-level interest
- Demo-to-qualified-lead: 3–5 leads per 10 demos
- Cost per lead: <$0.10 at scale (Phase 3)
- Cold email response rate: ~1%
- First follow-up response rate: 21%
- Right tone can improve response rates by 20%

**Engagement Metrics**:
- Demo duration: 15–30 minutes
- Discovery depth: 10–15 minutes minimum
- Follow-up sent within 24 hours: 100%
- Total touches before close: 6–9 (max 3–5 per channel)
- Send volume for serious B2B growth: 1,000+ emails daily requires ~18 domains
- 20,000 emails monthly with 3% reply rate + 20% meeting conversion = ~120 meetings

## Anti-Patterns

**ICP & Targeting**:
- Targeting 13,500+ vague prospects (too broad)
- Assuming all customers equally ideal
- Ignoring product analytics and churn data
- Over-filtering by location without accounting for remote workers
- Setting company size too broadly

**Outreach & Messaging**:
- "Pitch-slapping" with full pitch in message 1
- Following up more than 3 times
- Treating outreach as one-shot opportunity
- Spray-and-pray volume (damages reputation)
- One-and-done emails (requires 6–9 touches)
- Generic templates without personalization
- Unverified email addresses
- Weak follow-ups without fresh value

**Demos**:
- One-way monologues (prospect <50% talk time)
- Feature tours without benefit connections
- Skipping discovery phase
- Showing all features (show 3–4 max)
- Justifying or arguing about pricing
- Failing to recap and reconfirm
- Generic, non-customized demos
- Demos exceeding 30 minutes
- Talking >50% of the time

**Infrastructure**:
- Sending from primary domain
- Manual mailbox setup (5+ days, lockouts)
- Inadequate list quality
- Neglecting domain authentication (SPF/DKIM/DMARC)
- No monthly audits of saved searches
