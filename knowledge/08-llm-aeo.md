# LLM SEO & AI Engine Optimization (AEO)

## Overview

LLM SEO and Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) are emerging channels for SaaS discovery, where AI models (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini) cite and recommend your product in generated answers. ChatGPT referrals convert **6x better** than Google search. Early-stage startups can appear in AI answers within weeks, not years. The playbook is fundamentally different from traditional SEO: citations matter more than clicks, distributed mentions trump single backlinks, and original research is now table-stakes.

## Frameworks

### The 5-Step AEO Funnel

1. **Build organic mentions** across Reddit, GitHub, Twitter, forums, reviews
2. **Establish SEO rankings** for relevant queries (strong traditional SEO enables AEO)
3. **Get cited in high-authority content** through guest posts and digital PR
4. **Monitor AI visibility metrics** with Brand Radar, Rankshift, GPTrends
5. **Track discovery attribution** by asking new users how they found you

### Content Types AI Loves to Cite

- Comparison pages ("Typeform alternative")
- Use-case guides and tutorials
- FAQs with clear H2/H3 hierarchy
- Case studies with original data/benchmarks
- Listicles and round-ups
- Help-center/technical documentation

**Defining requirement**: Original insights. Could a competitor replicate this tomorrow? Answer should be "no."

### The Tokenization Model

LLMs compress language into tokens. Consistent brand mentions across *distributed sources* (Reddit, GitHub, LinkedIn, reviews, news) are statistically more likely to be retrieved than a single authoritative backlink. **Implication**: Build share of voice across 5-10 channels, not just your domain.

## Playbook

### Week 1-2: Audit & Foundation

- Audit current presence in ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity using Brand Radar or manual testing
- Create 10-15 test prompts mimicking real buyer questions (e.g., "free Typeform alternative," "AI form builder")
- Map visibility gaps: where competitors appear, you don't
- Implement technical SEO: crawlability, Schema.org/JSON-LD markup, no JavaScript-rendering barriers
- Set up tracking: Google Search Console, Bing Webmaster Tools, GA4 segments for chat.openai.com/perplexity.ai referrers

### Week 3-6: Core Content (2-3 pieces/week)

- Create "definitive sources" on frontier concepts (low-competition, high-authority topics)
- Include: original data, benchmarks, code blocks, tables, diagrams
- Structure clearly: H1→H2→H3 hierarchy for chunk-level LLM retrieval
- Avoid: promotional language, hype, generic advice, AI-generated filler
- Formats: comparisons, use-case guides, FAQs, tutorials, case studies

### Week 7-12: Distribution & Authority

- **Backlinks & Digital PR**: Guest posts, expert commentary in authoritative publications, award submissions
- **Social proof**: 4-6 LinkedIn posts/week, authentic Reddit engagement 1x/week, proprietary community platforms
- **Citation seeding**: Share original research in forums, link in GitHub/Stack Overflow/Hacker News
- **Help-center investment**: Optimize internal docs—can become 1-2 highest-ROI content pieces
- **Community participation**: Post authentically in niche forums; build grassroots recommendations (avoid spam)

### Ongoing (Monthly)

- **Content refresh**: Update pieces at 30, 90, 180-day intervals (stale content stops being retrieved)
- **Visibility benchmarking**: Monthly Brand Radar check, prompt testing, competitor position changes
- **Weekly quick wins**: (1) Benchmark, (2) Publish/update 1 asset, (3) Secure 1 new mention, (4) Engage in 2-3 communities
- **Ask for data**: In onboarding, ask "How did you discover us?" Include ChatGPT as an option

## How LLMs Choose What to Recommend

1. **RAG model (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)**: ChatGPT/Copilot use Bing's index; Google uses its own; Perplexity uses a mix. Content must be crawlable and structured.

2. **Distributed authority matters more than PageRank**: Multiple high-signal sources (Reddit, GitHub, LinkedIn, news, reviews) outweight a single domain authority.

3. **Original data beats citations**: LLMs detect and deprioritize hyper-optimized SEO copy, AI-generated content, and generic material. Authentic expertise with proprietary insights gets retrieved.

4. **Recency signals**: Content refreshed at 30/90/180-day intervals continues to be retrieved; stale content drops out.

5. **Structural clarity**: Clear headings (H1→H2→H3), single-idea paragraphs, and Schema.org markup improve chunk retrieval and citation likelihood.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Treating AEO as separate from SEO**: Strong traditional SEO doesn't auto-convert to AEO; you need distribution and citations.
- **Waiting for massive content volume**: 5-10 "definitive source" pieces beat 100 generic blog posts. Quality > quantity.
- **Assuming keyword stuffing works**: LLMs interpret meaning, not keywords. Synonym swapping and hyper-optimization backfire.
- **Spam in communities**: Aggressive self-promotion gets banned. Reddit and forums crawled by AI—authenticity is the distribution channel.
- **One-and-done publishing**: Stale content stops being retrieved. Require 30/90/180-day refresh cadence.
- **AI-generated content**: Models detect and devalue it. Write like a human expert.
- **Ignoring help-center/docs**: Internal documentation is often one of the highest-ROI content pieces for AI citations.
- **Not tracking AI discovery**: If you don't ask new users "How did you find us?", you won't know if AEO is working.
