# Idea Validation

## Overview
Idea validation separates the 20% of founder intuition worth building on from the 80% that sounds good in your head. This is about speed and cost-efficiency: build a $20 prototype before spending $20K on infrastructure. The core principle is "people do > people say" — test with real usage, not hypothetical feedback.

## Frameworks

### Hypothesis Scoring
1. List all beliefs about customers, market, and product
2. Cap at 25 hypotheses max (focus)
3. Score each: **Impact (1-10) × Uncertainty (1-10) = Hypothesis Score**
4. Prioritize high-scoring hypotheses addressing business-critical unknowns

Example: "If I write weekly valuable content, 30% of subscribers will share it" (measurable, cause-and-effect)

### Early Customer Profile (ECP)
Select your beachhead segment by these four characteristics:
1. Burning pain point requiring immediate solution
2. Sufficient willingness to pay (non-negotiable for sustainability)
3. Proximity to your network (realistic market penetration)
4. Case study potential (for social proof and upmarket expansion)

### SWOT Analysis
Time: 3 hours. Assess market opportunities and threats against your strengths/weaknesses. Provides competitive position clarity.

### Market-Problem Map
Visualize target customer's problem space to guide decisions and nurture empathy. Use Miro for collaborative planning.

### Value Proposition (UVP + USPs)
Bridge gap between customer's painful current state and desired future state.
- **UVP**: "Get actionable go-to-market assets in seconds" (single, compelling benefit)
- **USPs**: Methodology tested on 8,000+ companies, trained on 1M+ proprietary knowledge (specific, defensible differentiators)
- Focus on: high-tactical use cases, low time-to-value (minutes/hours, not weeks), high-frequency requests

### Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity
Ask these four parallel questions to find optimal pricing:
1. "At what price would you reject this entirely?" (too expensive)
2. "At what price would quality concerns emerge?" (too cheap)
3. "At what price does it become worth considering?" (high side)
4. "At what price is it an obvious bargain?" (good value)

Output: Chart showing acceptable price band where "expensive" and "good value" lines intersect.

### Bias Detection
Before launching any survey or conducting interviews:
1. List all assumptions about the product idea
2. Identify emotional attachments to specific features
3. Trace belief origins: gut feeling vs. customer conversation vs. data
4. Rewrite survey questions to eliminate leading language
5. Include "not interested" option for all core premises

### Relative Preference Methodology
Instead of checkboxes, ask: "Select ONE most interesting option, select ONE least interesting option." Reveals true priorities vs. artificial inflation.

## Playbook

**Week 1: Foundation (Days 1-2)**
1. Document 25 hypotheses about customer/market/product, ranked by Impact × Uncertainty
2. Run SWOT analysis (3 hours)
3. Define Early Customer Profile with 4 criteria
4. Map customer problem space (Market-Problem Map)

**Week 1-2: Research & Interview (Days 3-5)**
1. Conduct market sizing:
   - Calculate TAM: (addressable market) × (3% market share assumption) × (monthly price point)
   - Map competitor landscape
   - Search volume for your solution keywords
   - Average customer spending in category

2. Run 5-20 customer discovery interviews:
   - Schedule 45-60 minutes per interview
   - Use audio-only calls (participants more open, less self-monitoring stress)
   - Offer $10-25 incentive per participant
   - Cover these 6 core dimensions:
     * How do they currently solve this problem?
     * How often does the problem occur?
     * How severe/costly is it?
     * Who else is involved (stakeholders, decision influencers)?
     * What have they already tried?
     * Current spending (money + time)

3. Use this interview opening: *"Walk me through what [problem] currently looks like for you?"*
   Then progress through: why they do it, how it's evolved, frequency, manual steps, current tools, tool adoption history, failed attempts, costs per tool, team members, external parties, least favorite aspects, magic wand question: *"If you could wave a magic wand, how would you improve this?"*

4. End with: *"Is there anything else I should know?"* (reaching-for-door question halfway through)

5. Always send personalized thank-you note separate from incentive

6. Build Value Proposition template aligned to discovered pain points

**Week 2-3: Validation Testing (Days 6-7+)**
1. Choose lowest-cost experiment matching your hypothesis:
   - **Landing page + waitlist**: email capture, measure 5% visitor-to-signup minimum
   - **Cold outreach to 40 niche professionals**: personalized LinkedIn messages, direct conversation
   - **Concierge MVP**: manually deliver service before building product
   - **Pre-sales conversations**: direct selling before building infrastructure
   - **Prototype MVP**: no-code chatbot or landing page tool

2. Deploy on warm channels first (LinkedIn, existing network) before cold outreach

3. Measure success:
   - Landing page: 5% visitor-to-signup
   - Cold outreach: 100% response rate to conversations; 75%+ willingness to pay
   - Pre-sales: 20%+ letter of intent signature
   - Prototype: 100+ serious users (30 minimum with 3+ interactions)
   - Users demand access when beta goes offline

## Templates

### Discovery Interview Script
**Starting Phase:**
- "Thank you for making time. Do you have any questions first?"
- Confirm incentive: ($10-25 gift card)
- Request recording permission if needed

**Core Questions:**
1. "Walk me through what [problem] currently looks like for you?"
2. How often does this happen?
3. What's the cost if you don't solve it (time, money, frustration)?
4. What have you tried to fix this?
5. How long have you used your current solution?
6. What prevents you from switching?
7. Who else should we talk to?
8. If you could wave a magic wand, how would you improve this?

**Closing:**
- "Is there anything else I should know?"
- Send personalized thank-you note

### Cold Outreach Email
**3-Element Structure:**
1. **Flattering** — Respectful of recipient's expertise
2. **Fair** — Honest about mutual benefit
3. **Respectful** — Acknowledging their time

Example result: 100% conversation acceptance, 75% willingness to pay ($50/month)

### Van Westendorp Pricing Survey
```
1. At what price is [product] too expensive? (slider/number)
2. At what price would you have quality concerns? (slider/number)
3. At what price does it become worth considering? (slider/number)
4. At what price is it an obvious bargain? (slider/number)
```

### Market Sizing Template
**Formula:** (Total addressable market) × (% your segment represents) × (monthly price point) = TAM

**Example (vegetarian restaurant software):**
- 11M restaurants in Europe × 3% vegetarian share = 330K addressable market
- At €100/month SaaS = €33M monthly TAM
- 10% market capture = €3.3M revenue potential

## Benchmarks
| Metric | Target | Context |
|---|---|---|
| Minimum interviews for confidence | 5-10 | Start with 5; expect 10-20 for pattern consistency |
| Interview conversion to willingness-to-pay | 75%+ | Critical success signal |
| Landing page visitor-to-signup | 5%+ | Demand validation baseline |
| Cold outreach response rate | 100% | From personalized, warm outreach to niche professionals |
| Cold outreach willingness-to-pay | 75%+ | Explicit pre-revenue commitment |
| Letter of intent signature rate | 20%+ | For B2B pre-sales validation |
| Prototype MVP minimum users | 100+ | With 30+ minimum having 3+ interactions |
| Interview incentive | $10-25 | Per participant (gift card, cash) |
| Interview duration | 45-60 min | Minimum 45; prefer 60 |

## Anti-Patterns

- **Building before validating** — Most common fatal mistake. Prototype first, build second.
- **Extensive upfront interviews (30+) without prototype iteration** — You'll hear what people say, not what they do.
- **Arbitrary budget allocation ($30K+) without validation** — Validate with $20 first.
- **Broad positioning across multiple use cases** — Narrow to one beachhead segment before expanding.
- **Launching to cold audiences before proven concept** — Deploy on warm channels (LinkedIn network, existing audience) first.
- **Combined survey questions** — "Are you a creator interested in courses?" mixes status with intent. Split into separate questions.
- **High minimum pricing in surveys** — Starting at $500+ filters out honest feedback. Use broader ranges: $150-300, $301-499, $500-1000.
- **Skipping thank-you notes** — Burns reputation for future research and interviews.
- **Video interviews over audio** — Video increases self-monitoring stress and biases responses. Use audio-only calls when possible.
- **Vague problem statements** — If 5 interviews reveal wildly different responses, your problem definition is too broad.
- **Checkbox completion bias** — "Having done product discovery" without acting on findings is worse than no validation.
- **Emotional attachment untraced to customer data** — Always trace belief origin: gut vs. conversation vs. data.
