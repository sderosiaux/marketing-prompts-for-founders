## Source: Failory - Building in Public

### Key Frameworks

**Channel-Founder-Market Fit Model**
- Identify platforms where target audience congregates (Channel-Market Fit)
- Choose formats and platforms you genuinely enjoy using (Channel-Founder Fit)
- This dual fit ensures sustainability and consistency

**Transparency Decision Matrix**
Stop being transparent when:
1. Competitors gain unfair data advantage
2. Copycat competitors emerge
3. Public pressure management becomes burdensome
4. Metrics become misleading without context

### Actionable Tactics

**Content Types to Share**
- Work-in-progress screenshots and demos
- Business metrics and milestone achievements
- Development/marketing/hiring strategies
- Bug fixes and feature launches
- Industry insights and obstacles overcome
- User feedback and testimonials
- Weekly progress updates

**Platform Selection by Product Type**
- Twitter: Tech/B2C founders (high virality)
- LinkedIn: B2B products (broad reach)
- Newsletters: Deep engagement with existing customers
- TikTok: B2C targeting younger demographics
- Niche Communities: Higher conversion-ready audiences
- Streaming (YouTube/Twitch): Low-friction documentation

**Consistency Requirements**
- Maintain regular cadence—extended gaps cause audience attrition beyond core customer base

### Templates/Swipe Files

**Weekly Standup Format**
Share daily accomplishments and next-week priorities (pattern demonstrated in Trends.vc community)

**Open Dashboard Pattern**
Publish transparency pages showing revenue, traffic, and metrics (examples: Buffer, Ghost, Pieter Levels)

### Metrics & Benchmarks

- Buffer established "default to transparency" as organizational value (2013)
- Pieter Levels grew 130K+ Twitter followers through sustained open sharing
- Justin Welsh accumulated 165K+ LinkedIn followers building digital products publicly
- Scott DeLong generated 13K+ newsletter subscribers after viral growth documentation

### Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- "Launch in public" then disappear (short-term strategy fails)
- Building across multiple channels simultaneously (unsustainable workload)
- Sharing on wrong platforms (TikTok for B2B, for example)
- Ignoring channel-founder fit (discomfort kills consistency)
- Over-interpreting raw metrics without business context

## Source: Mr Runlocked - Founder-Led LinkedIn Guide

### Key Frameworks

**3 Pillars of LinkedIn Success**
1. Founder-led content (personal authority building)
2. Employee-led content (team narrative contribution)
3. Company content (strategic institutional messaging)

**7-Step LinkedIn Strategy Foundation**
- Ideal Customer Profile & Jobs-To-Be-Done definition
- Positioning & messaging framework
- Founder brand & story development
- Thought leadership positioning
- Goal-setting (2-3 prioritized objectives)
- Dark social tracking (self-reported attribution)
- Content pillar structure (up to 5 core topics)

**TOFU-MOFU-BOFU Content Model**
- TOFU (Top of Funnel): Awareness-stage content with broad reach
- MOFU (Middle of Funnel): Consideration-stage insights and education
- BOFU (Bottom of Funnel): Conversion-stage proof, case studies, objection handling

**95/5 Ratio Principle**
Most prospects aren't actively buying. Build trust long before sales outreach through consistent value delivery rather than direct sales pitches.

### Actionable Tactics (10 Key Approaches)

1. **Value-Commenting**: Leave thoughtful comments on target audience posts (2-3 daily minimum)
2. **Strategic List-Building**: Categorize connections (influencers, ICP, customers, cheerleaders, peers, mentors)
3. **Social Selling Mindset**: "Stop trying to sell. Start genuinely helping"
4. **Cold Campaign Strategy**: Send 200 connection requests weekly with personalized, relevant messaging
5. **Warm Lead Activation**: Prioritize profile visitors, post engagers, and connection requests
6. **5-Phase Pipeline**: Connection → Offer Help → Qualification → Meeting → Follow-up
7. **New Signup Engagement**: Connect immediately with demo bookers and free trial users
8. **Featured Section CTAs**: Display lead magnets, free trials, or demo booking options prominently
9. **Comment Response Strategy**: Reply to post comments within 30-60 minutes to boost organic reach
10. **Peer Amplification**: Maintain 3-5 "ICP supporters" who comment on posts early for algorithmic boost

### Content Creation Process

**Weekly Interview Method** (30-45 minutes per session)
- Prepare 7-10 thought-provoking prompts
- Record and transcribe conversation
- Extract key stories and frameworks
- Write posts maintaining founder voice
- Publish 3-5 times weekly

**10 Example Prompt Categories**
- ICP mistakes listicles
- Industry trend reactions
- Personal career lessons
- Future role predictions
- Problem-solving frameworks
- Sales call trend observations
- Sales FAQ responses
- Client case studies
- Objection handling
- Tech stack breakdowns

### Recommended Tools

- **Content Management**: Notion, Trello
- **Video Recording**: Riverside, Descript
- **Post Ideation**: Kleo, Taplio
- **Writing/Structuring**: AuthoredUp, Taplio, Aware
- **Prospecting**: LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Favikon
- **Analytics**: Aware, Taplio, Shield

### Metrics & Benchmarks

- **Posting Frequency**: 3-5 posts weekly minimum for sustained visibility
- **Engagement Allocation**: 20+ minutes daily on commenting and community interaction
- **Content Ratio**: Balance TOFU, MOFU, BOFU strategically
- **Time Investment**: Adam Robinson example—21 million LinkedIn views at $4M ARR but required "65% of his time on content work"

### Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Relying solely on company page engagement
- Sporadic posting (less than 3x weekly)
- Generic, AI-generated comments
- Immediate hard selling in outreach
- Ignoring warm signals (profile visits, engagement)
- Pursuing vanity metrics (likes, follows) over pipeline
- Inconsistent founder voice/delegated authenticity
- No clear self-attribution tracking mechanism

### Key Success Stories

- Adam Robinson (21M views, $4M ARR)
- Enzo Avigo (building in public with humor)
- Tyler Denk

## Source: MarkePear - LinkedIn CEO/Founder Playbook

### Key Frameworks

**"One Word to Own"**
Select a trigger word/phrase (e.g., "AI tools for marketing") to become top-of-mind when relevant conversations occur

**Content Pillars (Archetypes)**
- Claim statements establishing positions
- Experiments documenting real results
- How-to/Q&A addressing audience pain
- Common obstacles acknowledging friction
- Advice with contrarian angles
- Expertise demonstrations from lived experience

**Build in Public Model**
- Share real-time progress, challenges, and wins with storytelling framing
- Create engagement loops through polls and feedback requests
- Update consistently while highlighting milestones and lessons

### Actionable Tactics

**Audience Building**
- Send 20/day connection requests (free) or 50/day (paid)
- Respond meaningfully to post comments—avoid generic replies
- Engage thoughtfully on other posts; consider respectful disagreement with established voices

**Content Creation Workflow**
Record 30-minute weekly calls with CEO/CTO asking:
- What happened this week?
- What surprised you?
- What angered you?

Extract 2-3 insights per session, starting with compelling hooks.

**Hook Strategy**
Spend ~50% of writing time on hooks.
- Text posts: 5 lines of hook
- Video/image posts: 3 lines of hook
- Use audience self-selection: "If you're a CMO at 1-5M SaaS startups, here's..."

**Giveaway Posts**
- Lead with results ("Exact playbook I used to...")
- Show scrolling GIF/video of resource
- Require connection/like/comment
- Gate behind email signup optionally

**Converting Engagement to Leads**
1. Identify engaged users from target audience
2. Send: "Thanks for engaging! We help with XYZ. Let's chat."
3. Offer gated resources (searchable Notion libraries, playbooks, templates)
4. Follow with DIY "sales letters"—tactical concepts ending in CTAs

### Tools & Resources Mentioned

**Agencies/Consultants**
- Project33 (founder-led marketing specialist)
- Alec Paul (thought leadership consultant)
- Devin Reed (ex-Gong, content strategist)
- Justin Gage (technical long-form, Hacker News specialist)

**Automation**
- Phantom + Zapier + Sheets
- CommonRoom (engagement monitoring)
- Gong (prospect tracking/attribution)

### Metrics & Measurement

**Don't measure vanity metrics (likes). Instead track:**
- Engagement quality (thoughtful comments, reposts)
- Target audience resonance (CTOs, relevant decision-makers discussing content)
- Conversions via self-reported attribution ("How did you find us?")
- Sales team feedback on prospect awareness

### Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Generic comment responses ("great," "amazing")
- Posting without clear hooks or strategy
- Ignoring the role of storytelling and authenticity
- Treating all profiles identically (each should have distinct personality)
- Measuring success by vanity metrics alone

## Source: Mr Runlocked - Social Listening Guide

### Key Frameworks

**Social Listening vs. Brand Monitoring**
- **Brand Monitoring**: Reactive tracking of direct mentions; responding to customer service inquiries
- **Social Listening**: Proactive identification of broader conversations about problems you solve, industry trends, and competitor discussions

**4-Step Implementation Process**
1. Identify channels where target audience congregates
2. Define keywords to track (brand, competitors, pain points, buying signals)
3. Monitor channels (manually or via tools)
4. Engage meaningfully with high-intent mentions

### Actionable Tactics

**High-Intent Signal Categories**
- Explicit problem statements ("struggling with X")
- Competitor dissatisfaction ("looking for DocuSign alternative")
- Feature requests or gaps
- Tool comparisons ("vs." searches)
- Budget/pricing complaints

**Engagement Best Practices**
- Provide value first before mentioning your product
- Disclose founder/team affiliation for transparency
- Move sensitive discussions to DMs
- Follow up when prospects show interest
- Edit AI-generated responses for personal voice

**Platform-Specific Strategies**
- **Twitter/X**: Use advanced search with Boolean operators for precision
- **Reddit**: Monitor niche subreddits (r/SaaS, r/selfhosted); use F5bot for alerts
- **LinkedIn**: Watch comment sections on influencer posts; search relevant groups
- **Hacker News**: Monitor "Ask HN" threads
- **Slack/Discord**: Join community channels and monitor discussions
- **Quora**: Track "best software for X" questions

### Keyword Strategy Template

Track these categories:
- Your brand name + variations
- Product category terms (e.g., "form builder," "e-signature tool")
- 3-5 competitor names
- Pain point phrases your ICP uses
- Buying signals ("recommend," "alternative to," "choosing," "looking for")
- Industry jargon relevant to your space

### Tools Mentioned

| Tool | Cost | Best For |
|------|------|----------|
| Google Alerts | Free | Broad web mentions; includes blogs/news |
| F5bot | Free | Reddit & Hacker News alerts |
| Octolens | Paid | B2B SaaS; AI filtering for buy-intent; multi-platform |
| Brand24/Mention | Paid | General brand monitoring |
| Brandwatch/Hootsuite | Paid (Enterprise) | Large-scale analytics & scheduling |

### Metrics & Benchmarks

- **Tally** (form builder): Reached 2M ARR on team of 5, bootstrapped—used social listening as primary acquisition channel instead of paid ads
- **Documenso** (open-source DocuSign alternative): Primary growth from Reddit (r/selfhosted, r/SaaS) and Hacker News engagement
- **Octolens**: Uses its own tool for customer acquisition, tracking 5-10 keywords across platforms

### Anti-Patterns to Avoid

| Mistake | Impact | Fix |
|---------|--------|-----|
| Direct pitching without context | Appears spammy; low conversion | Contribute value first |
| Hiding founder affiliation | Erodes trust, especially on Reddit | Disclose transparently |
| Wrong keyword selection | Miss opportunities or drown in noise | Track competitors, pain points, buying intent |
| Using unedited AI responses | Reads generic; lacks authenticity | Add personal expertise & POV |
| LinkedIn-only focus | Miss high-intent communities | Monitor Reddit, HN, Discord, Slack groups |
| Sporadic checking | Miss time-sensitive opportunities | Use automated tool or calendar recurring blocks |

### Secondary Benefits (Beyond Acquisition)

- **Content inspiration**: Identify trending topics and language your audience uses
- **Product roadmap input**: Track feature requests and pain points mentioned publicly
- **Competitive intelligence**: Monitor how competitors are discussed; identify weaknesses
- **Messaging refinement**: Adopt your audience's terminology; address common objections upfront
- **User-generated content**: Collect testimonials and authentic endorsements for social proof

## Synthesis: Cross-Source Patterns & Consolidated Intel

### Unified Frameworks

**The Authentic Authority Model** (Meta-Framework)
Across all platforms, the pattern is consistent:
1. **Choose one platform** you genuinely enjoy (Channel-Founder Fit)
2. **Establish expertise** through consistent, valuable content (3-5 posts/week minimum)
3. **Engage authentically** before asking for anything (95/5 rule: 95% value, 5% ask)
4. **Track quality signals**, not vanity metrics (conversions, target audience resonance, pipeline)
5. **Maintain discipline** through systematic daily routines (1 hour/day engagement)

**The Account Warmup Pattern** (Platform-Specific)
Before posting, all algorithm-driven platforms benefit from 3-8 days of:
- Account optimization without publishing
- Algorithm training and signal calibration
- Community observation and listening
- This applies to: LinkedIn, Twitter, TikTok, Pinterest, Instagram

**The Value-First Engagement Sequence**
Across communities (Reddit, HN, LinkedIn, Discord):
1. Contribute genuine value without selling (comments, answers, help)
2. Build credibility and trust within the community
3. Disclose affiliation transparently
4. Only mention your product when it directly solves a stated problem
5. Move deeper conversations to DMs

### Consolidated Actionable Tactics

**Daily Time Allocation (1 Hour Total)**
- 15-20 min: Read/observe content in your niche
- 15-20 min: Comment thoughtfully on 5-10 existing posts/discussions
- 15-20 min: Publish your own content (if on publishing schedule)
- 5-10 min: Respond to engagement on your content

**Content Creation Process** (Weekly)
- Record 30-45 min interview/conversation with cofounder or customer
- Extract 2-3 key insights/stories from the conversation
- Write 3-5 posts from those insights (different angles, formats)
- Spend 50% of writing time on hooks/opening lines
- Publish 3-5x per week across chosen platform(s)

**Lead Generation from Social** (High-Intent Approach)
1. Monitor social channels for high-intent signals using F5Bot or similar tools
2. Identify "struggling with X" or "looking for alternative to Y" mentions
3. Provide value-first response without selling
4. If helpful, DM with: "Thanks for engaging! We help with [exact problem]. Let's chat."
5. Send gated resource (template, playbook, case study)
6. Follow up with tactical "sales letter" containing CTA

**Platform-Specific Content Strategies**
- **LinkedIn**: Mix TOFU (awareness) + MOFU (education) + BOFU (conversion) content 1x/week each
- **Twitter/X**: Combine announcements + education + memes + community celebration; prioritize visuals
- **Reddit/HN**: Answer questions, contribute solutions, build community reputation first; mention product only when relevant
- **Quora**: Answer high-view, low-answer questions to capture search traffic ("parasite SEO")
- **Communities (Discord/Slack)**: Monitor discussions, contribute expertise, respond to problems naturally

### Consolidated Anti-Patterns

**Critical Mistakes** (Across All Platforms)
1. ❌ Posting sporadically or inconsistently (less than 3x/week kills algorithm reach)
2. ❌ Pursuing vanity metrics (followers, likes) instead of pipeline and conversions
3. ❌ AI-generated or generic engagement ("great post!" comments)
4. ❌ Selling before establishing credibility in community
5. ❌ Ignoring channel-founder fit (forcing yourself onto platforms you hate)
6. ❌ Delegating founder voice to others (removes authenticity)
7. ❌ Posting immediately after account creation (triggers shadowbans and throttling)
8. ❌ Building across 5+ channels simultaneously (unsustainable; focus on one first)

**Platform-Specific Traps**
- **LinkedIn**: Relying on company page instead of founder accounts; sporadic posting; vanity metrics obsession
- **Twitter**: Threading excessively; low-quality memes; algorithm-chasing instead of audience-serving
- **Reddit**: Self-promotion and spam (communities punish this); hiding affiliation; wrong subreddit selection
- **All platforms**: Unedited AI responses; hidden monetization; inauthentic formatting

### Recommended Growth Sequence for Founders

**Month 1: Foundation**
- Choose ONE platform where target audience congregates
- Optimize profile with clear value prop and CTAs
- Spend 1 week observing (no posting yet)
- Start daily commenting/engaging ritual
- Identify 3-5 content pillars specific to your expertise

**Month 2-3: Consistency**
- Publish 3-5x per week on chosen platform
- Maintain 1 hour daily engagement routine
- Record weekly founder conversations
- Extract 2-3 insights per conversation

- Set up social listening for 5-10 keywords relevant to ICP

**Month 4-6: Optimization**
- Analyze which content types generate conversions (not just likes)

- Activate warm leads from profile visitors/engagers
- Build 3-5 "amplifier" relationships with complementary creators
- Track self-reported attribution to measure impact

**Month 7-12: Leverage**
- Expand to secondary platform if comfortable
- Activate employee advocacy (2-3 team members following founder framework)
- Formalize content calendar with templates
- Begin systematizing lead nurture from social insights
- Measure revenue impact and ROI on time investment

### Final Consolidated Insight

**The solo founder trap**: Believing 10,000 followers = guaranteed growth. Reality: 100 high-quality inbound conversations from target ICPs = measurable pipeline.

**The winning approach**: One platform. Consistent 3-5x/week publishing. 1 hour daily engagement. Real conversations with target market. Self-reported attribution tracking. Patient 6-12 month runway to see real traction.

Success doesn't require virality—it requires consistency, authenticity, and laser focus on your actual customer conversation, not follower counts.
