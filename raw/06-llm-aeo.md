## Source: GTMStrategist — How to Win the New SEO Game: AI Search

### Key Frameworks

**Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)** - Optimizing content for AI tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google's AI Overviews, not just traditional search.

**Search Everywhere Optimization** - Expanding from single-channel SEO to multichannel presence across platforms and formats to build brand authority.

**Tokenization** - How LLMs compress and process language, making consistent brand mentions across the internet statistically more likely to appear in AI responses.

### Actionable Tactics

**1. ICP Definition Study**
- Conduct video meetings with C-level, sales, customer support teams
- Interview end clients
- Analyze business data: surveys, Google Ads, brand materials, sales calls
- Create comprehensive ICP documentation for content strategy

**2. Technical SEO Fundamentals**
- Ensure flawless site crawlability and accessibility
- Implement structured data (Schema markup)
- Remove JavaScript-rendered content that's invisible to crawlers
- Add internal linking from blog posts to product pages

**3. Content Distribution (Backlinks & Digital PR)**
- Secure expert commentary backlinks in authoritative publications
- Pursue digital PR placements in news outlets
- Attend industry events and conferences
- Guest appearances on podcasts
- Win industry awards

**4. Social Media Activity**
- Post 4-6 times weekly on LinkedIn (heavily crawled by AI)
- Build expertise positioning to become a quotable authority
- Leverage LinkedIn content that ranks in Google and AI Overviews

**5. On-Page Optimization for AI**
- Use clear H2/H3 heading structures for "chunk-level retrieval"
- Create self-contained, single-idea paragraphs
- Add unique value (proprietary data, field experience)
- Include citations and authoritative references
- Add Author/Organization schema markup
- Use plain, factual language (avoid promotional tone)

**Bonus: Community SEO**
- Build active Reddit presence
- Create proprietary forums (customer communities indexed by search)
- Follow Shopify's model: host user-generated content platforms

### Key Metrics & Benchmarks

- **Traffic Growth**: 5x daily organic traffic achieved in case study
- **Lead Growth**: 30% increase in daily leads
- **Speed to Rankings**: Featured in AI Overviews #1 in less than 24 hours
- **Content Volume**: ~20k keywords researched; 5k-keyword lists sufficient for most cases

**Critical Insight**: "Traffic has become a much less relevant metric. Rankings and mentions have instead taken a much stronger position." Lower traffic doesn't prevent customer acquisition—invisible rankings do.

### Tools Mentioned

- Ahrefs Brand Radar (track brand visibility across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google's AI Overview)
- Aleyda Solis's AI Search Optimization Checklist
- LinkedIn as primary B2B platform for capturing AI search mentions

### Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Treating search as isolated channel (fails in multichannel AI era)
- Over-relying on content creation without distribution strategy
- Ignoring technical SEO foundations
- Using promotional language instead of factual positioning
- Skipping social proof and expert positioning

## Source: Vercel Blog — How We're Adapting SEO for LLMs and AI Search

### Key Frameworks

**Traditional SEO vs. LLM SEO Comparison**
- Diverge on backlinks vs. embedding-based relevance, SERP rankings vs. RAG visibility
- Overlap on crawlability, heading hierarchy, and fresh content

**RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) Model**
- Systems fetch external information at runtime
- ChatGPT, Copilot, Meta AI use Bing's index; Google uses its own; Perplexity uses a mix
- Content must be crawlable and structured for effective retrieval

### Actionable Tactics

**1. Find Frontier Concepts**
- Identify low-competition, high-opportunity topics by monitoring Twitter/X, Reddit, GitHub, Discord
- Become the authoritative source with original data or benchmarks

**2. Publish Definitive Sources**
- Go beyond surface-level coverage with metrics, code blocks, tables, diagrams, and original insights
- "Could a competitor replicate this tomorrow?" should be answerable with "no"

**3. Structure for Machines**
- Use consistent terminology, H1→H2→H3 hierarchies, Schema.org/JSON-LD markup, semantic HTML
- Avoid JavaScript-heavy implementations that prevent crawling

**4. Seed Authentic Citations**
- Share in forums, create open-source resources, and build interlinked topic clusters
- Focus on high-signal channels (Reddit, GitHub, Hacker News, Stack Overflow)

### Key Metrics & Benchmarks

- ChatGPT referrals grew from 1% (6 months prior) to 10% of new Vercel signups
- Tally experienced "AI search become their biggest acquisition channel," driving growth from $2M to $3M ARR in 4 months
- Google AI Overviews may reduce clicks by up to 34.5% vs. non-AI results

**Tracking Signals**
- Source citations in Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT
- Referrer traffic from chat.openai.com, perplexity.ai, bard.google.com, claude.ai
- Index coverage via Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools
- Core Web Vitals scores for indexing performance

### Tools Mentioned

- Schema.org and JSON-LD markup
- Next.js with Server-Side Rendering (SSR), Static Site Generation (SSG), or Incremental Static Regeneration (ISR)
- Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools
- Analytics platforms (Vercel Analytics, Ahrefs, Mention, Semrush)

### Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Keyword stuffing and synonym swapping lack impact; "LLMs don't match keywords; they interpret meaning"
- Hidden text and legacy tactics muddy signals or harm traditional SEO
- Neglecting traditional SEO entirely for LLM-only optimization
- Stale content stops being retrieved; refresh cadence matters at 30, 90, and 180-day intervals

## Synthesis: Unified AEO & LLM SEO Strategy

### Core Mental Models (Across All Sources)

**1. Shift From Click-Based to Citation-Based Metrics**
- Traditional SEO optimizes for ranking position on a search results page
- AI search optimizes for being cited/recommended within AI-generated answers
- **Implication**: Traffic and clicks are no longer the primary success metric; mentions, citations, and visibility in AI syntheses are

**2. Tokenization & Distributed Authority**
- LLMs process language through tokenization, making consistent brand mentions across distributed sources statistically more likely to be retrieved
- A single authoritative backlink (traditional SEO) is less valuable than diverse mentions across 5-10 sources (AEO)
- **Implication**: Build "share of voice" across multiple platforms, not just your own domain

**3. Content Quality Threshold Has Risen**
- AI models detect and devalue: hyper-optimized SEO copy, AI-generated content, generic/derivative material
- Original research, proprietary benchmarks, and authentic expertise are now table-stakes
- **Implication**: The difference between "good enough" and "gets cited" is original data, metrics, or insights competitors can't replicate overnight

### Unified Playbook: The 12-Week AEO Launch

#### Week 1-2: Audit & Foundation
- [ ] Audit current presence in ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity (use Brand Radar or manual testing)
- [ ] Create 10-15 test prompts that mirror your buyers' real questions
- [ ] Map visibility gaps: where do competitors rank for these prompts, and you don't?
- [ ] Implement technical SEO: ensure crawlability, add Schema.org markup, fix JavaScript rendering issues
- [ ] Set up tracking: Google Search Console, Bing Webmaster Tools, analytics for referrer traffic from AI sources

#### Week 3-6: Content Foundation (2-3 pieces/week)
- [ ] Create "definitive sources" on frontier concepts (low-competition, high-authority topics)
- [ ] Formats that AI loves: comparisons, use-case guides, FAQs, tutorials, case studies, listicles
- [ ] Each piece must answer: "Could a competitor replicate this tomorrow?" (answer should be "no")
- [ ] Include: original data, benchmarks, code blocks, tables, diagrams, clear H2/H3 hierarchy
- [ ] Avoid: promotional language, hype, generic advice, AI-generated filler

#### Week 7-12: Distribution & Authority
- [ ] **Backlinks & Digital PR**: Guest posts, expert commentary, reviews, award submissions
- [ ] **Social Proof**: 4-6 LinkedIn posts/week, Reddit weekly engagement, proprietary community platforms
- [ ] **Citation Seeding**: Share in forums, link in GitHub, Stack Overflow, Reddit, Hacker News
- [ ] **Help-Center Investment**: Optimize internal docs; becomes 1-2 of highest ROI content pieces
- [ ] **Community Presence**: Post authentically 1x/week in niche forums; build grassroots recommendations

#### Ongoing (Monthly Cadence)
- [ ] **Content Refresh**: Update pieces at 30, 90, 180-day intervals (stale content stops being retrieved)
- [ ] **Visibility Benchmarking**: Monthly check-in on Brand Radar score, prompt testing, competitor position changes
- [ ] **Weekly Quick Wins**: (1) Benchmark, (2) Publish/update 1 asset, (3) Secure 1 new mention, (4) Engage in 2-3 communities
- [ ] **Ask for Data**: In onboarding flows, ask "How did you discover us?" Include ChatGPT as option

### Critical Success Factors (Why Most Fail)

1. **Treating AEO as separate from SEO** — Both matter. Strong SEO doesn't automatically convert to AEO; you need distribution AND citations.

2. **Waiting for massive content volume** — 5-10 "definitive source" pieces beat 100 generic blog posts. Quality over quantity.

3. **Ignoring community as a distribution channel** — AI crawls Reddit, GitHub, Twitter, reviews. Authentic community presence is a citation source, not spam opportunity.

4. **Not tracking AI discovery** — If you don't ask, you won't know. Requires explicit tracking (surveys, UTM tags, referrer analysis).

5. **Publishing once and expecting results** — Stale content stops being retrieved. Refresh at 30, 90, 180-day intervals.

6. **Forgetting that LLMs detect inauthenticity** — AI-generated content, hyper-optimized copy, and generic material are flagged and devalued. Write like a human expert.

- [ ] **Week 3**: Publish your first "definitive source" piece (not a blog post—something with original data/insight)
- [ ] **Week 4+**: Systematically work through the 12-week playbook above
