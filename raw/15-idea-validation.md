# Idea Validation: Extracted Marketing Intel

## Source: 7-Day Business Idea Validation Framework (GTM Strategist)

### KEY FRAMEWORKS

**1. SWOT Analysis (Day 1)**
- Assess market opportunities and threats against personal strengths/weaknesses
- Described as "still works like a charm" for rapidly identifying competitive position
- Time: 3 hours

**2. Early Customer Profile (ECP) Selection (Day 2)**
Four critical characteristics for selecting beachhead segment:
- Burning pain point requiring immediate solution
- Sufficient willingness to pay (critical for sustainable business model)
- Proximity to your network enabling realistic market penetration
- Case study potential for social proof and upmarket expansion

**3. Market-Problem Map (Day 2)**
- Developed by Simon Belak
- Visualizes target customer's problem space to guide decision-making and nurture empathy
- Tool: Miro for collaborative planning

**4. Value Proposition Template (Day 3)**
- Bridge gap between customer's current painful state and desired future state
- Focus on: highly tactical use cases, low time-to-value (measurable in minutes/hours, not weeks), high-frequency service requests
- Provides UVP vs. USP distinction:
  - **UVP**: "Get actionable go-to-market assets in seconds"
  - **USPs**: Methodology tested on 8,000+ companies, trained on 1M+ characters of proprietary knowledge, free to test

**5. Competitive Alternatives Scoring (Day 4)**
- Rate competitors across evaluation criteria on 1-5 scale
- Identifies where your solution uniquely wins

**6. Validation Methods Framework (Day 5)**
- By Dr. Else van der Berg, adjusted for rapid testing
- Prioritize product tests and marketing tests over traditional interviews

### ACTIONABLE TACTICS

**Core Principle**: "People do > People say" — Test with real usage, not hypothetical feedback

**7-Day Timeline & Time Allocation:**
| Day | Activity | Time | Output |
|-----|----------|------|--------|
| 1 | Market analysis | 3 hours | Vision clarity |
| 2 | Define ECP | 3 hours | Target profile |
| 3 | Value prop refinement | 3 hours | Core UVP + USPs |
| 4 | Competitive positioning | 3 hours | Differentiation strategy |
| 5 | Validation method selection | 3 hours | Testing approach |
| 6 | Build prototype | 3 hours | Functional beta |
| 7 | Launch & analyze | 3 hours | User insights |

**Total commitment: 21 hours, $20 budget**

### METRICS & BENCHMARKS

- **User sample size target**: 100+ serious users (minimum 30 with 3+ message exchanges)
- **Conversation depth metric**: 418 chats = 3,724 messages over one week
- **Cost**: $20 (Chatbase subscription for AI chatbot prototype)
- **Development time**: 3 hours to prototype with no-code builder
- **Launch channel**: LinkedIn soft launch (no email blast or influencer amplification)
- **Success signals (Day 7)**: 15 users demanded product access when beta went offline; 2 users expressed willingness to pay

### TOOLS MENTIONED

- **Chatbase** — AI chatbot builder for prototype validation ($20)
- **Google Slides Value Proposition Template** — Free template by Paweł Huryn & Aatir Abdul Rauf
- **Miro** — Collaborative tool for Market-Problem Map creation
- **LinkedIn** — Launch and soft validation channel

### ANTI-PATTERNS

- **Avoid extensive upfront interviews** (30+) without prototype iteration
- **Avoid arbitrary budget allocation** ($30K+) without validation
- **Avoid broad positioning** across multiple use cases before narrowing focus
- **Avoid launching to cold audiences** before proven concept
- Imposing traditional methodologies and fictional timelines on validation process (instead: speed, cost-efficiency, eat your own dogfood with existing audience knowledge)

## Source: Idea Validation Framework (Mr RunUnlocked)

### KEY FRAMEWORKS

**5-Step Validation Process**

**Step 1: Document Assumptions & Hypotheses**
- Write down beliefs about customers, market, and product without evidence
- Create testable hypotheses using cause-and-effect structure with measurable goals
- Example: "If I write weekly valuable content, 30% of subscribers will share it"
- Cap hypotheses at 25 to maintain focus

**Step 2: Rank Hypotheses (Hypothesis Score Method)**
- Formula: **Impact (1-10) × Uncertainty (1-10) = Hypothesis Score**
- Prioritize high-scoring hypotheses addressing business-critical unknowns first

**Step 3: Market Research & Sizing**
Key questions to answer:
- Market size and growth rate
- Competitor landscape
- Search volume for your solution
- Average customer spending

Example calculation (vegetarian restaurant software):
- 11M restaurants in Europe × 3% vegetarian share = 330K addressable market
- At €100/month SaaS = €33M monthly TAM
- 10% market capture = €3.3M revenue potential

**Step 4: Customer Interviews**
Critical questions:
- "What are your 3 biggest problems in [category]?"
- "How are you solving this currently?"
- "What prevents you from changing?"
- "How would your ideal scenario look?"

Key principle: Understand customer pain before pitching your solution.

**Step 5: Run Low-Cost Experiments**

Five MVP types:
1. **Concierge MVP** – Manually deliver service before building product
2. **Landing Pages** – Test with email signups, waitlists, or pre-orders
3. **Physical Prototypes** – Low-fidelity versions for hardware
4. **Pre-sales** – Direct conversation to gauge purchase intent
5. **Crowdfunding/Product Hunt** – Test market interest

### ACTIONABLE TACTICS

**Success Metrics** (define upfront):
- 100 B2B customer waitlist signups
- 1,000 pre-orders
- 20% of interviewed companies sign letter of intent
- 5% visitor-to-signup conversion rate

**Validation Confidence Spectrum** (from lowest to highest confidence):
1. Market research (easiest)
2. Customer interviews
3. Real money from customers (hardest)

### ANTI-PATTERNS

- Building product immediately without validation is "one of the most common and painful mistakes" early founders make

### METRICS & BENCHMARKS

- TAM calculation example: 11M restaurants × 3% = 330K addressable market; at €100/mo = €33M TAM
- Success signal: 20% of interviewed companies signing letter of intent
- Conversion benchmark: 5% visitor-to-signup on landing page

## Source: How to Not Do Product Discovery (Demand Maven)

### KEY FRAMEWORKS

**Bias Detection Framework**
Identify confirmation bias by asking yourself:
- What do I already believe about customer value, usage patterns, and feature requirements?
- Where did these beliefs originate (actual customer data vs. assumptions)?
- What ideas am I emotionally attached to that haven't been validated?

**Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity Method**
Use these four parallel questions to establish optimal pricing ranges:

1. **Too Expensive**: At what price would you reject the product entirely?
2. **Too Cheap**: At what price would quality concerns emerge?
3. **High Side**: At what price does it become worth considering?
4. **Good Value**: At what price is it an obvious bargain?

Output: Chart identifying acceptable price band (where "expensive" and "good value" lines intersect)

**Relative Preference Methodology**
- Select ONE most interesting option
- Select ONE least interesting option
- Analyze results to identify priorities

Advantage: Reveals true preferences vs. artificial inflation from checking all boxes

### ACTIONABLE TACTICS

**Survey Design Pitfalls to Avoid:**

**Anti-Pattern: Combined Questions**
- Don't merge creator status with intentions into single options
- Result: Leading answers that don't reflect reality

Better Approach: Split into separate questions
- First: Creator status (full-time, newer, team member, aspiring)
- Second: Specific business goals

**Anti-Pattern: Assumptive Pricing Questions**
- Starting with high minimum prices ($500+) filters out honest feedback
- Creates false positives about willingness-to-pay

Better Approach: Use broader price ranges ($150-300, $301-499, $500-1000)

**Six Critical Missing Discovery Questions:**
1. **Learning Preference Validation**: Which education method resonates (courses, podcasts, workshops, books)?
2. **Prior Experience**: Have you purchased creator courses before?
3. **Desired Outcomes**: What would success look like after completing this?
4. **Future State**: How would your life/business improve?
5. **Dealbreakers**: What would prevent you from buying?
6. **Interview Willingness**: "Would you participate in a follow-up interview?"

**Pre-Survey Self-Audit Process:**
1. List all assumptions about the product idea
2. Identify emotional attachment to specific features
3. Trace belief origins (gut feeling vs. customer conversation vs. data)
4. Rewrite survey questions to eliminate leading language
5. Include "not interested" options for all core premises

### ANTI-PATTERNS

| Issue | Impact | Solution |
|-------|--------|----------|
| No need qualification | Assuming desire without validation | Ask: "Are you interested in X first?" |
| Hidden assumptions | Survey respondents select options that don't match reality | Split compound questions |
| Incomplete price data | Missing nuance between $150-$500 range | Add intermediate price bands |
| No medium validation | Assuming course format is wanted | Ask learning preference explicitly |
| Limited context | High-level feedback without depth | Plant interviews early in survey |
| **Checkbox completion bias** | Having "done product discovery" doesn't equal meaningful insights; poor methodology can be worse than no validation | Validate methodology before launching |

## Source: Pre-Product Validation Case Study: Jason Cohen & WP Engine (Marketing Examples)

### KEY FRAMEWORKS

**Pre-Product Validation Process:**
1. Identify target audience (WordPress consultants)
2. Reach out directly via professional network
3. Present the problem you're solving
4. Gauge genuine interest before building

### ACTIONABLE TACTICS

**Cold Outreach Strategy:**
- Search for 40 relevant professionals in your niche
- Personalize contact through professional networks (LinkedIn emphasized)
- Focus on solving their specific pain points

**Email Characteristics for Success:**
The email succeeded because it was:
- Flattering (respectful of recipients' expertise)
- Fair (honest about mutual benefit)
- Respectful (acknowledging their time)

### METRICS & BENCHMARKS

- **Response rate**: 100% conversation acceptance (40 outreach = 40 conversations)
- **Conversion rate**: 75% willing to pay ($50/month) — 30 out of 40 professionals
- **Pre-revenue customer acquisition**: Before building infrastructure
- **Outcome**: Validated approach led to $100M/year business (WP Engine)

### ANTI-PATTERNS

- Building infrastructure, branding, or presentation materials before validating market demand
- Jason reversed conventional path: customers came first, company name and PowerPoint came later

## Source: Customer Discovery Interview Script (Deploy Empathy)

### KEY FRAMEWORKS

**Core Interview Goals — Validate Problem Existence:**
1. **Problem existence** — Do target users experience this issue?
2. **Frequency** — How often does the problem occur?
3. **Severity** — How painful/costly is it?
4. **Current solutions** — What have they tried?
5. **Current spending** — Money and time investments
6. **Stakeholders** — Who else is involved internally/externally?

### ACTIONABLE TACTICS

**Minimum Interview Count:** 5 interviews to start, but expect 10-20 before patterns emerge consistently

**Interview Duration:** Schedule 45 minutes minimum, preferably 1 hour

**Step-by-Step Interview Process:**

**Starting Phase:**
- Confirm identity and thank them
- Invite their questions first
- Confirm incentive details ($10-25 gift card)
- Request recording permission if needed

**Core Questions (Meaty Phase):**
- Opening: *"I'm interested to learn more about how you [problem]. Can you walk me through what that process currently looks like?"*

Then progress through:
- Why the company performs this process
- How the process has evolved
- Frequency of occurrence
- Manual steps involved
- Current tools/solutions used
- Tool adoption timeline and decision-making
- Failed tool attempts and why
- Cost per tool
- Decision influencers outside their team
- Team members involved in decision
- External parties involved
- Least favorite aspects
- **Magic wand question**: "If you could wave a magic wand, how would you improve this?"

**Closing Phase:**
- Use "reaching for the door" question halfway through: *"Is there anything else I should know?"*
- Always send personalized thank-you note separate from incentive

**Recruitment Tactics — Where to Find Participants:**
- Reddit communities (with specific recruitment templates)
- Facebook groups
- Twitter
- LinkedIn
- Discord/Slack communities

**Key messaging for recruitment:**
- Make incentive explicit upfront
- Clearly state it's research, not a sales pitch
- Emphasize no obligation to purchase

### METHODOLOGY NOTES

**Interview Format:** Audio-only calls preferred over video (participants more open, reduces self-monitoring stress)

**Optional Enhancement:** Request screensharing of current process, but acknowledge offline/approval elements may not surface

**Scope Warning:** If 5 interviews reveal wildly different responses, your problem definition is too broad

### ANTI-PATTERNS

- Attempting to sell during discovery interviews
- Skipping the thank-you note (always send one)
- Vague problem statements leading to inconsistent responses
- Insufficient interview sample size (minimum 5, preferably more)
- Over-relying on video calls when audio would be more effective

### METRICS & BENCHMARKS

- **Minimum interviews**: 5 to start
- **Target interviews**: 10-20 for pattern consistency
- **Interview incentive**: $10-25 gift card
- **Interview duration**: 45 minutes minimum, 1 hour preferred

## Synthesis: Best Practices Across All Sources

### UNIFIED VALIDATION PROCESS (Consolidated Timeline)

**Week 1: Foundation & Hypothesis (Days 1-2)**
- Document all assumptions and hypotheses about customer, market, and product
- Cap hypotheses at 25 and score by Impact × Uncertainty
- Conduct SWOT analysis to clarify competitive position
- Define Early Customer Profile with 4 core characteristics:
  - Burning pain point
  - Sufficient willingness to pay
  - Proximity to your network
  - Case study potential

**Week 1-2: Research & Interview (Days 3-5)**
- Conduct market sizing: TAM calculation, competitor landscape, search volume, spending patterns
- Run 5-10 customer discovery interviews (minimum 5, preferably 10-20 for pattern confidence)
- Use critical discovery questions: current pain, frequency, severity, current solutions, spending, stakeholders
- Interview format: 45-60 minutes, audio-only preferred, $10-25 incentive per participant
- Build Market-Problem Map and Value Proposition template aligned to customer pain

**Week 2-3: Validation Testing (Days 6-7+)**
- Choose lowest-cost experiment matching your hypothesis:
  - Landing page with signup/waitlist (tests demand signal)
  - Concierge MVP (manually deliver service first)
  - Pre-sales conversations (direct intent validation)
  - Cold outreach to 40 niche professionals (direct response validation)
- Target metrics: 5% visitor-to-signup conversion, 20%+ letter of intent signature, 75%+ willingness to pay signals
- Deploy on warm channels first (LinkedIn, existing network) before cold outreach

### KEY FRAMEWORKS (Ranked by Application Order)

1. **Hypothesis Scoring** (Impact × Uncertainty) — Prioritize what to test
2. **SWOT Analysis** — Understand competitive position
3. **Early Customer Profile (ECP)** — Define beachhead segment with 4 criteria
4. **Market-Problem Map** — Visualize customer pain landscape
5. **Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity** — Test pricing willingness
6. **Bias Detection Framework** — Audit survey/interview methodology before launching
7. **Relative Preference Ranking** — Get true priorities (not inflated checkboxes)
8. **Cold Outreach with Social Proof** — Validate demand through direct conversation

### CRITICAL SUCCESS METRICS (By Validation Stage)

**Problem Validation:**
- Consistent pain identification across 10-20 interviews (patterns, not outliers)
- 75%+ interviewed customers express willingness to pay something

**Demand Validation:**
- Landing page: 5% visitor-to-signup minimum
- Cold outreach: 100% response rate to conversations; 75%+ willingness to pay
- Pre-sales: 20%+ letter of intent signature from interviewed companies
- Prototype MVP: 100+ serious users (30 minimum with 3+ interactions)

**Traction Validation:**
- Users demand access when beta ends
- Explicit payment commitment before building full product

### METHODOLOGIES TO PRIORITIZE (Ranked)

1. **Cold outreach with warm research** (40 professionals in niche, personalized)
2. **Customer discovery interviews** (10-20 with script covering 6 core dimensions)
3. **Landing page/waitlist** (landing page → email capture → direct outreach for interviews)
4. **Concierge MVP** (manually deliver service before building tech)
5. **Pre-sales conversations** (direct selling before building)
6. **Prototype validation** (no-code chatbot, landing page tool)

**Avoid:**
- Building before validating (most common fatal mistake)
- Extensive formal surveys without interview depth (checkbox bias)
- Broad positioning before narrow segment validation
- Emotional attachment to ideas without customer data origin-tracing
- Combined survey questions (split into separate items)
- High minimum pricing in surveys ($500+; use broader ranges: $150-300, $301-499, $500-1000)
