# Product Hunt Launch Intel

## Source: Lenny's Newsletter - How to Successfully Launch on Product Hunt

### KEY FRAMEWORKS

**Goal-Setting Framework**
- Prioritize conversion metrics (signups, trial conversions, paid) over ranking position
- Define success metrics before launch day, not after

**30-Day Warm-Up Timeline**
- Begin audience engagement 30 days pre-launch
- Build anticipation among supporters
- Encourage Product Hunt account creation among supporters early
- Identify high-value users who will engage

**Time Zone Staggering Strategy**
- Segment outreach into three waves:
  - 12:01 a.m. PT (launch moment)
  - 7 a.m. PT (U.S. morning engagement)
  - 2 p.m. PT (afternoon momentum)
- Maintains steady engagement velocity throughout the day

### ACTIONABLE TACTICS

**Pre-Launch Preparation**
- Have supporters create PH accounts early (algorithm favors established accounts)
- Focus on high-karma users within your ideal customer profile
- Engage Maker Network weeks before by supporting other launches
- Create comprehensive launch-day war plan timeline

**Launch Day Execution**
- Assign 2-3 team members for comment responses (batch every 2 hours)
- Schedule 10 email blasts across time zones
- Embed live upvote counter on your website to create FOMO
- Respond to every comment on launch day

**Day Selection Strategy**
- Mondays/Fridays: Lower competition, better for ranking chances
- Tuesday-Thursday: Highest traffic but fiercest competition
- Weekends: Best statistical chance at #1 ranking

**Algorithm Optimization**
- Maintain consistent vote velocity rather than spiking early
- Prioritize quality comments from high-karma users
- Flag suspicious new accounts as spam to protect your ranking
- Don't expect spikes—steady momentum wins

**Critical Assets Development**
- Tagline: "Compelling and clear," avoid vague language like "turbocharge"—use action verbs
- Product Screenshots: Show actual UI with embedded captions explaining value (minimum 3 images)
- Demo Video: Simple Loom walkthrough preferred over polished 4-week productions (3-5 minutes, posted to YouTube)
- Maker's Comment: Tell origin story addressing pain points, explain differentiation, include clear CTA with discount code

### METRICS & BENCHMARKS

**Time Investment**
- 50-120 hours typical for successful Product Hunt launches

**Conversion Expectations**
- B2B Signups: 50-300 considered successful
- B2C Signups: 500-1,500 average; 3,000+ for broad appeal products
- Long-tail benefit: High-authority PH backlink provides ongoing SEO value

### ANTI-PATTERNS

- **Launch parties/simultaneous voting**: Triggers spam filters and shadow bans
- **Bot/fake account strategies**: Can result in deranking from Product Hunt
- **Upvoting rings**: Recent algorithm changes penalize coordinated voting groups
- **Launching too polished**: "Founders often launch too late" (Rajiv Ayyangar, PH CEO)
- **B2B without self-serve onboarding**: Early adopters expect immediate product access without sales calls
- **Ignoring momentum maintenance**: Spikes in early voting can trigger anti-spam filters

## SYNTHESIS: Best Practices Across Sources

### Core Success Formula
1. **Pre-launch prep (30 days out)**: Build audience, encourage account creation, warm up Maker Network
2. **Strategic timing**: Choose day carefully (weekends best for ranking, weekdays for traffic)
3. **Launch day execution**: Coordinate 2-3 team members, maintain 10+ email blasts, batch comment responses every 2 hours
4. **Sustain momentum**: Keep vote velocity steady (not spiking), prioritize quality engagement from high-karma users

### What Actually Matters
- Conversion metrics > ranking position
- High-karma user engagement > volume of supporters
- Steady momentum > early spike
- Simple demo video > perfect polish
- Clear tagline with action verbs > clever/vague copy
- B2B needs self-serve onboarding to capitalize on traffic

### Critical Launch Assets (Priority Order)
1. Strong tagline with action verb
2. 3+ product screenshots with captions
3. Simple demo video (3-5 min Loom walkthrough)
4. Origin story + differentiation in maker comment with CTA

### Time & Resource Expectations
- Budget 50-120 hours for complete launch campaign
- Assign 2-3 people minimum for launch day
- Expect 50-300 signups (B2B) or 500-3,000+ (B2C)

### What to Absolutely Avoid
- Fake voting schemes or upvoting rings
- Launch parties that coordinate voting
- Polished launches (launch earlier and iterate)
- B2B products without self-serve access
- Aggressive early voting spikes (triggers spam filters)
