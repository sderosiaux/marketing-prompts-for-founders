# Paid Ads Marketing Intelligence for SaaS Founders

## Source: Mr. Run Unlocked - Google Ads Guide

### Key Frameworks

**Readiness Checklist for Google Ads**
- Clear Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) defined
- Market category identified
- Target audience actively searching online
- Defined Unique Selling Points (USPs)
- Daily budget of €25-50+ available for testing

**Account Setup Framework**
1. Install Google Tag Manager (GTM) for centralized tag management
2. Set up Google Ads Conversion Actions (form submissions, demo requests, trial signups)
3. Enable Enhanced Conversion Tracking using encrypted first-party data
4. Implement Google Consent Mode V2 (mandatory for EU/UK as of March 2024)
5. Track Offline Conversions connecting CRM (HubSpot) to track MQLs, SQLs, customers

**Account Structure Best Practices**
- Max 2 campaigns initially; segment by keyword type (competitor vs. product category)
- 1 Ad Group per creative theme aligned with website navigation structure
- Use Responsive Search Ads (RSAs) with 15 headlines, 4 descriptions
- Begin with 1 RSA per ad group to let Google identify winning combinations

**4 Campaign Types (Priority Order)**
1. Competitor Keywords: "[Competitor] alternative," "[Competitor] competitors"
2. Product Category Keywords: "Sales automation software," "AI research tools"
3. Use Case Keywords: "Transcribe Zoom calls," "Automate lead nurturing"
4. Brand Keywords: Your brand name, variations, misspellings

### Actionable Tactics

**Budget Allocation Formula**
For €9,000/month budget:
- 60-70% (€4,900) → High-intent product category & use case keywords
- 20-25% (€2,300) → Remarketing & brand campaigns
- 20-30% (€1,800) → Competitor keywords

**Keyword Research Process**
1. Use Google Ads Keyword Planner (free)
2. Input competitor URLs for inspiration
3. Analyze competitor strategies via Ad Transparency Center
4. Check G2/Capterra for category listings
5. Interview sales/customer service teams for pain point keywords
6. Build keyword matrix mapping keywords → campaigns → ad groups

**Negative Keywords Strategy**
Exclude low-intent terms:
- "Free [product] tools"
- Generic informational keywords
- Non-target industries/use cases

**Remarketing Strategy**
- Target: Users who visited site in past 30 days without converting
- Channel: Display network, Gmail ads
- Creative: Comparison pages, case studies
- Setup: Connect GA4 to Google Ads + enable Consent Mode

### Templates

**Competitor Campaign Ad Copy**
- Headline: "Top-Rated [Competitor] Alternative"
- Description: "Rated 4.9 on Capterra for Ease of Use. Explore..."

**Product Category Campaign Ad Copy**
- Headline: "The #1 [Category] Tool – Trusted by 10,000+ Teams"
- Description: "[Brand] is trusted by 10k+ teams. Try 14-Days Free"

**Use Case Campaign Ad Copy**
- Headline: "AI Meeting Transcription?"
- Description: "Instantly Generate Call Summaries. Sync Into Your CRM..."

**Brand Campaign Ad Copy**
- Headline: "[Brand] – 10,000+ Teams Rely on Us"
- Description: "Record, transcribe, summarize [product] effortlessly. Start free trial today!"

### Tools Mentioned

- Google Tag Manager (GTM)
- Google Ads Keyword Planner
- Google Ads Ad Transparency Center
- Google Consent Mode V2
- GA4 integration
- Google Ads Scripts for monitoring trending search terms
- Looker Studio (free reporting templates)

### Metrics & Benchmarks

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Click-Through Rate (CTR) | 3-5% |
| Conversion Rate (CVR) - Demo/MQL | 1-1.5%+ |
| Conversion Rate - Free Trial | 7%+ |
| Cost-Per-Acquisition (CPA) | Set based on LTV; €120 example for high-intent |

**Account Readiness**
- Daily budget minimum: €20-25 (under €20/day not recommended)
- Requires 15 RSA headlines = 300,000+ impressions needed for full evaluation

### Anti-Patterns

1. Poor conversion tracking (relying on GA4 imports instead of offline CRM integration)
2. Over-segmentation (too many campaigns/ad groups without sufficient data)
3. Underestimating budget (competitive categories need €50-500/day minimum)
4. Too many ad creatives early (300k+ impressions required for evaluation)
5. Ignoring trademark issues (using competitor brand names without legal review)
6. Not using competitor names where applicable without trademark clearance
7. Using navigational keywords for competitor campaigns (login, dashboard, billing)

## Source: GTM Strategist - The No-Fluff LinkedIn Ads Playbook

### Key Frameworks

**The 12-Week Experiment Model**
Structure LinkedIn ads as a time-bound test rather than ongoing spend:
- Duration: 3 months provides sufficient data while demanding strategic rigor
- Budget calculation: (Target results × Target CAC) = Total budget
- Example: 200 demo signups × $100 CAC = $20,000 investment

**The GTM Motion Fit Assessment**
Evaluate LinkedIn ads readiness with three questions:
1. Is product-market fit validated?
2. How do LinkedIn ads integrate into existing acquisition channels?
3. Does the campaign serve early-funnel leads or deal-closing?

**The BBB Rule for Top-of-Funnel Creative**
- High-contrast Background for visual dominance
- Bold headline communicating single benefit
- Single Benefit (one USP per ad)

### Actionable Tactics

**Campaign Structure**
- Single experiment campaign with one ad set containing 5-6 weekly rotating creatives
- Pause previous week's ads before launching new ones to force algorithm recalibration
- Once positive ROAS achieved: split into 90% main campaign + 10% experiment budget

**Audience Targeting**
- Target niche audiences of 10-50k people (opposite of Meta's broad approach)
- Base targeting on job roles and seniority levels
- Reverse exclude audiences (e.g., exclude 51+ employee companies instead of targeting 1-50)
- Disable audience network expansion

**Bidding Strategy**
- Use manual bidding with cost caps
- Start 50% below LinkedIn's recommended bid
- Increase bid by 10% every 24-48 hours until reaching full daily spend

**Creative Testing Schedule**
- Weeks 1-6: Launch new ad creatives weekly
- Week 7+: Test audiences and campaign types with top performers

**High-Performing Creative Elements**
- Large, bold numbers (stats/metrics) outperform generic imagery
- Avoid stock photos and product dashboard screenshots
- Single-image ads with clear value proposition before testing video
- Customer testimonials and case studies for bottom-of-funnel

### Templates

**Creative Testing Roadmap**
- Weeks 1-6: Launch new ad creatives weekly
- Week 7+: Scale top 20% performing creatives to audiences and campaign types
- Maintain creative rotation to force algorithm recalibration

**Reporting Template Structure**
Track per creative:
- Impressions, clicks, CTR
- Conversions and conversion rate
- Cost per conversion (CPC)
- ROAS progression

### Tools Mentioned

- Figma Library (155+ LinkedIn ad examples for inspiration)
- Google Sheets (reporting template for tracking creative performance)
- LinkedIn Insight Tag (conversion event tracking)
- UTM tags (internal tracking)

### Metrics & Benchmarks

**LinkedIn-Specific Benchmarks**
- Average CPC: $15-$100 range (platform expensive vs. other channels)
- Audience size sweet spot: 10-50k for initial testing
- Conversion funnel assumption: Ad click (15% rate) → Landing page (22% rate) → Conversion
- Key insight: Ad creatives contribute approximately 80% of campaign performance

**Success Indicators**
- Positive ROAS signals readiness to scale (after 12-week testing)
- Never sole high-ROI channel; complement existing acquisition methods

### Anti-Patterns

1. Running 1-week campaigns with <$500 budget
2. Testing multiple variables simultaneously (message + layout + audience)
3. Creating separate ad sets for each creative (dilutes budget allocation)
4. Using automatic audience expansion
5. Splitting budget across too many GTM channels before mastering 1-2
6. Mixing top-funnel and bottom-funnel creatives in single campaign
7. Generic copy and overwhelming product screenshots
8. Failing to pause previous ads when launching new experiments

- Consideration (30% budget): 90-day website/video viewers, customer quotes/third-party reviews/partnerships/awards, Goal: Solution consideration
- Conversion (15% budget): 30-day website visitors, time-limited offers/impact metrics/direct CTAs, Goal: Trial/demo signups

**10 Campaign Types & Ad Formats**
1. Single Image (versatile demand gen, feed-native)
2. Carousel (product features/testimonials, multiple clickable images)

5. Spotlight (personalized awareness, dynamic profile-based)
6. Message (direct engagement, inbox conversation starter)
7. Conversation (multi-option responses, inbox-only non-EU)
8. Event (registrations, requires LinkedIn event setup)
9. Document (lead capture, in-feed content consumption)
10. Follower (company page growth, right column desktop)

### Actionable Tactics

**Step-by-Step Campaign Setup**

**Step 1: Target Audience**
- Preferred: Upload company lists via CSV or CRM integration
- Minimum audience size: 3,000 (if highly targeted)
- Negative audiences: customers, competitors, irrelevant industries/roles

**Step 2: Conversion Tracking**
- Hard conversions: Trial signup, demo booked, deals closed
- Soft conversions: CTA clicks, pricing page visits, content downloads
- Windows: Hard (90-day click / 7-day view); Soft (7-day click / 1-day view)
- Track each hard conversion twice (last-touch campaign AND last-touch overall)

**Step 3: Landing Page Optimization**
- Address audience pain point
- Position solution
- Show product features (functional proof)
- Include social proof (testimonials, G2/Capterra reviews)
- Provide clear offer
- Mobile-optimized design

**Step 4: Creative Principles**
- Formats: 4:5 for images, 1:1 for video
- Use high-performing organic posts as sponsored content
- Include emotion, humor, raw content
- Stop the scroll on mobile-first feeds

**Step 5: Performance Benchmarks Validation**
- Single image: CTR > 0.3%, dwell time > 3 seconds, quality score > 5
- Video: View rate > 30%, completion rate > 10%

**Step 6: Budget Calculator Formula**
CPM × audience size × target frequency (7 in 30 days) ÷ reach percentage (40-50%)

**Critical Settings Fixes**
- Disable LinkedIn Audience Network
- Turn off audience expansion
- Switch to manual CPC (reduce floor bid 10%)
- Use "recent" location targeting
- Understand AND/OR targeting logic

**Early-Stage Startup Strategy (≤$3K/month)**
- Focus 100% on remarketing audiences
- Use well-performing organic posts as ads
- Cover all awareness/engagement/conversion journey stages
- Maintain campaigns minimum 5 weeks; avoid early shutdowns

## Synthesis: Cross-Source Insights

### Universal Frameworks

**The Time-Bound Experiment Model**
Multiple sources converge on treating paid ads as structured experiments:
- LinkedIn: 12-week experiment structure with calculated budget
- Google Ads: 3-6 month commitment for meaningful data
- Common principle: Set duration upfront, measure against clear KPIs, avoid early shutdowns

**Multi-Stage Campaign Structure**
All sources recommend funnel-based allocation:
- **Awareness (50-55%)**: High reach, problem identification
- **Consideration (30%)**: Social proof, comparison, solution validation
- **Conversion (15-20%)**: Direct CTAs, limited offers, demos/trials

This 50-30-20 split appears across Google Ads (competitor/category/use case distribution) and LinkedIn (awareness/consideration/conversion budget allocation).

**Audience Targeting: Niche Over Broad**
- LinkedIn: 10-50k optimal audience size; reverse exclude rather than include
- Google Ads: Segmented by keyword intent (competitor/category/use case)
- Core principle: Precision beats broad reach at early stages

**Conversion Tracking as Foundation**
All platforms emphasize conversion tracking setup BEFORE campaign launch:
- Hard conversions: demo/trial/deal closed
- Soft conversions: page visits, content downloads
- Offline tracking: Connect CRM for true business outcomes (MQL, SQL, customer)
- Key requirement: Track twice (last-touch campaign AND overall last-touch)

### Universal Anti-Patterns

1. **Under-budgeting**: Minimum $3K-5K/month over 3+ months; under $500/week campaigns fail
2. **Testing too many variables at once**: Change one variable per experiment batch
3. **Poor creative execution**: Stock photos, product screenshots, generic copy underperform; use metrics, testimonials, raw/emotional content
4. **Incomplete setup**: Missing conversion tracking, audience expansion enabled, wrong bidding strategy
5. **Early shutdowns**: Campaigns need minimum 30 days to show first conversions; 3-6 months for pipeline visibility
6. **Sole-channel dependency**: Paid ads are complementary, not standalone

### Creative Performance Hierarchy

**Top Performers** (across all platforms):
- Large, bold numbers/metrics
- Customer testimonials and quotes
- Before/after comparisons
- Problem-centric headlines
- Clear, single benefit (BBB rule)

**Bottom Performers**:
- Stock photography
- Product dashboard screenshots
- Generic value propositions
- Overly salesy copy
- Multiple CTAs in single ad

### Metrics to Track Universally

**Minimum reporting metrics**:
- Impressions, clicks, CTR
- Conversion rate (soft and hard)
- Cost per conversion (CPC/CPA)
- ROAS (revenue or pipeline value)

**Benchmarks**:
- Google Ads: CTR 3-5%, conversion 1-1.5%+, CPA €120-500 depending on category
- LinkedIn: CPC $15-100, CTR 0.3%+ (single image), 30%+ view rate (video)
- Timeline: 30 days first conversions, 3-6 months pipeline visibility

### Budget Calculation Formula (Unified)

For any platform: **(Target conversions × Target CAC) = Campaign budget**

Examples:
- LinkedIn: 200 demos × $100 CAC = $20,000
- Google Ads: 40 customers × $500 LTV-based CAC = $20,000

### Platform-Specific Strengths

**Google Ads**: Best for bottom-funnel, high-intent keyword capture (demo/trial)
- Readiness: Search volume proof, $25-50/day budget, clear ICP
- Advantage: Intent-driven keywords, competitor targeting, remarketing

**LinkedIn Ads**: Best for B2B awareness and mid-funnel (decision-maker identification)
- Readiness: Product-market fit, $3K/month, 1,000+ followers, complementary channels
- Advantage: 65M+ decision-makers, account-based targeting, native content formats

**Key difference**: Google targets "what people search," LinkedIn targets "who people are" (role, company, seniority).

### Resource Checklist for First Campaign

**Pre-Launch**:
- [ ] Conversion tracking installed (GTM + CRM integration)
- [ ] Landing page optimized (pain point → solution → proof → offer)
- [ ] Audience defined (ICP, roles, companies, OR exclusions)
- [ ] Budget calculated (conversions × CAC = spend)
- [ ] Creative assets prepared (5-6 variations minimum)
- [ ] Reporting template created

**During Campaign (Weeks 1-6)**:
- [ ] Weekly creative rotations (pause old, launch new)
- [ ] Daily bid adjustments (increase 10% every 24-48h until full spend)
- [ ] Monitor CTR, conversion rate, cost per conversion
- [ ] Collect performance data before optimization

**After 30 Days**:
- [ ] First conversions should appear
- [ ] Identify top 20% performing creatives
- [ ] Scale winners; pause bottom performers

**After 3-6 Months**:
- [ ] Pipeline visibility established
- [ ] ROAS positive signals readiness to scale
- [ ] Consider channel diversification or creative expansion
